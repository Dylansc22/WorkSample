Mitigating Global Sedimentation Risk with Forest Infrastructure

A Draft Worksample by Dylan Scott Cobean
Natural Infrastructure, or Green Infrastructure, such as forests and wetlands have been shown through research to create massive benifits in some of the most important water management issues. Foresty and Water Management issues have been known to be related, but the public is beginning to better understand how important Forests are in regulating Water Management Issues.

Forests reduce downstream flood risk, improve infiltration rates, and capture sediment that would otherwise be carried downstream. Sedimentation risk in particular can be a factor for Water Treatment Plants and Hydroelectric Power Facilities.

This proof-of-concept worksample, looks at a number of environmental indicators and calculates an global erosion risk scores (by nation and by watershed). By overlaying that resulting data with WRI's publically available Global Forest Conditions, we can map regions around the global where targeted forest conservation and forest restoration efforts can mitigate risk to watersheds and urban environments.
